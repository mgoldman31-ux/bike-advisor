# Product Brief: Faster That Bike

## Problem
There is a lot of bike data out there - manufacturers publish detailed geometry tables for every model - but no single site aggregates it, and no data tool interprets the numbers. When considering a next bike (as the correct number of bikes is n+1), riders are left piecing together metrics across brand sites, paying for expert fitting, or scrolling through pages of forum opinions. 

## Goal
Users should find eligible bikes to meet their cycling goals, while building intuition for geometry metrics.

## Users
- Cyclists of all levels: the tool will explain why the key metrics matter, so all users can make a data-informed decision on their next bike.
- Cycling data enthusiasts or professionals: data visualizations showing aggregated geometry metrics will be useful in understanding the current industry landscape

## Why?
Last summer while I was not working, I finally had the time to bike! The descent into cycling obsession is steep: first there's the endorphins and feeling of accomplishment after a long ride. Next, what starts as a need to be able to fix a flat tire while on the road quickly becomes a need to understand every piece of this beautiful machine. And then come the speed gains, which might be limited by the weight of the bike and its measurements. The $850 Liv Avail road bike I bought four years ago no longer supports my cycling goals. I want to determine the perfect second bike while learning what data can predict for us.

## In scope (v1)
1. Bike comparisons according to index scores.
2. Recommended bikes determined by user's measurements and cycling goals (planned) 

## Out of scope / future
1. Tracking changes to geometry metrics within brands and across the industry
2. Showing data of past models no longer available on brand sites
3. Review database + LLM to interpret
4. Component comparisons
